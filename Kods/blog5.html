Sea turtles are among the oldest inhabitants of the oceans, having existed for over 100 million years. Their presence in today’s seas is a testament to nature's resilience and strength.

These majestic creatures swim thousands of kilometers to breed and lay eggs. Their role in the ecosystem is diverse – turtles help maintain the health of coral reefs and disperse seeds of marine plants.

Sadly, human activities such as pollution and fishing nets threaten their survival. To protect sea turtles, it is vital to focus on sustainable fishing practices and waste management.

By raising awareness and supporting conservation efforts, we can ensure that these ancient travelers continue to grace our oceans for generations to come.
