Green chameleons are true wonders of nature. Their ability to change color not only captures human imagination but also aids their survival in the wild. Chameleons use this unique skill for camouflage and emotional communication with others.

These reptiles are mostly found in Africa and Madagascar, feeding primarily on insects, which they catch with astonishingly fast and sticky tongues. Chameleons are also highly sensitive to changes in their environment, making them important ecological indicators.

Chameleon populations are threatened by climate change and habitat loss. Protecting these extraordinary creatures helps preserve biodiversity and our understanding of the complexity of nature.
